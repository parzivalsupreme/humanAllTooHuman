1. **Manuel "Manny" Villar** – **$17.2 billion**  
    A former politician turned real estate magnate, Villar chairs Vista Land & Lifescapes, one of the country's leading integrated property developers. His ventures also include Starmalls and Golden MV Holdings.
    
2. **Enrique Razon Jr.** – **$11.1 billion**  
    Razon is the chairman and CEO of International Container Terminal Services Inc. (ICTSI), a global port management company. He also has significant investments in the gaming and hospitality sectors.
    
3. **Sy Siblings** – **$13 billion**  
    Heirs to the late Henry Sy Sr., they control the SM Group, encompassing retail (SM Supermalls), banking (BDO Unibank), and property development (SM Prime Holdings).
    
4. **Ramon Ang** – **$3.8 billion**  
    As the president and CEO of San Miguel Corporation, Ang oversees one of the Philippines' largest and most diversified conglomerates, with interests ranging from food and beverages to infrastructure.
    
5. **Isidro Consunji & Siblings** – **$3.3 billion**  
    They lead DMCI Holdings, a conglomerate with businesses in construction, real estate, mining, and power generation.
    
6. **Tony Tan Caktiong** – **$3.2 billion**  
    Founder and chairman of Jollibee Foods Corporation, Tan Caktiong transformed a small ice cream parlor into a global fast-food empire. He also co-chairs DoubleDragon Properties.
    
7. **Andrew Tan** – **$2.6 billion**  
    Chairman of Alliance Global Group, Tan's portfolio includes real estate (Megaworld), food and beverage (Emperador), and gaming (Resorts World Manila).
    
8. **Lance Gokongwei & Siblings** – **$2.4 billion**  
    They helm JG Summit Holdings, with interests in air transportation (Cebu Pacific), telecommunications, banking, food manufacturing, and real estate.
    
9. **Lucio Tan** – **$2.3 billion**  
    Tan's conglomerate, LT Group, spans tobacco, alcohol, banking, and aviation, including Philippine Airlines.
    
10. **Dennis Anthony & Maria Grace Uy** – **$2.2 billion**  
    Founders of Converge ICT Solutions, they capitalized on the growing demand for high-speed internet, expanding their fiber-optic network across the country.
