
## Begin with the Objective Function

At base, trading is a capital‑allocation game under uncertainty.

**Goal:** Maximise long‑run, risk‑adjusted geometric growth of capital (not raw hits).

**Implication:** Every decision must be evaluated by its expectancy $$\text{Edge} = \text{Expectancy} - \text{Costs} - \text{Behavioural Drag}$$ and its impact on drawdowns.

Position sizing frameworks such as the **Kelly criterion** formalise that trade‑off.

## What Moves Price – Supply, Demand & Microstructure

Price is the clearing point where the marginal buyer meets the marginal seller; it wiggles because information, liquidity and psychology are constantly shifting.

Studies of order‑flow, liquidity pockets, volatility clustering and market regimes give the discretionary trader a “big‑air” feel for when conditions are benign versus treacherous.

Accept that most price movement is noise; the job is to **recognise the small subset of moments when the odds briefly tilt.**

## Discretion edge

*Harding Loevner* groups edge into: **informational**, **analytic**, **decision‑making**, and **organisational**.

For a discretionary trader, the practical edges are usually:

| Edge Type       | First-Principles Angle                                  | Action Items                                                             |
|------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Informational** | You see/hear faster or differently than the crowd.       | Build niche newsfeeds, talk to domain experts, monitor order‑flow.       |
| **Analytic**      | You process the same data but separate signal from noise better. | Develop repeatable pattern‑recognition templates; back‑test them.        |
| **Decision-making** | You execute cleanly when others freeze.                 | Ritualise entries/exits; pre‑define kill‑switches.                       |
| **Organisational** | Your routine reduces friction and fatigue.              | Automate journaling, use checklists, schedule deliberate review blocks. |

## Behavioural Drag

Cognitive and emotional biases erode expectancy just as surely as wide spreads. Key biases to neutralise include:

- Confirmation bias  
- Overconfidence  
- Loss-aversion  
- FOMO

### First‑principles fixes:

- **Awareness:** Know your personal “tilt” triggers (e.g., sleep, P&L swings).  
- **Process:** Use checklists, pre‑commitment stop levels, and mandatory pause before overrides.  
- **Environment:** Position size small enough that fear/greed never hijack the limbic system.

## Fuse Intuition with Data Validation

Pure gut feel is brittle; pure quant can miss nuance.  
Modern discretionary masters run a hybrid loop:

**Idea → Lightweight back‑test or replay → Trade → Post‑mortem → Refine rule‑of‑thumb**

## Anti‑Fragility

Markets change. Protect the franchise by diversifying both **method** and **lifestyle**:

- Keep multiple uncorrelated play‑books (e.g., mean‑reversion intra‑day & swing trend‑follow).
- Maintain physical and mental health routines — sleep and blood sugar beat any indicator.
- Hold ample liquidity to survive regime shifts; **longevity itself is a competitive edge.**
