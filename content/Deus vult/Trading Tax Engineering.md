# LEGAL Tax Minimisation for Prop-Firm Traders in the Philippines

## Goal
Pay the **least** amount of **legal** tax on trading profits 

---

## First Principles 

Break tax systems into 3 levers:
1. **Who** is taxed – change taxpayer status
2. **What** is taxed – recharacterize income
3. **How** it's taxed – use the smallest applicable rate

---

## 1. WHO is taxed — Change Tax Residency

### Principle
Philippine tax law only taxes **worldwide income** if you’re a **resident citizen**.  
If you become a **non-resident citizen (NRC)**, only your **Philippine-source** income is taxed.

### Loophole A: Become a Non-Resident Citizen (NRC)
- Stay abroad **more than 183 days per year** — the main BIR audit trigger.
- Maintain **foreign ties** (lease, bank, prop-firm payouts abroad).
- File **BIR Form 1701** as NRC for paper trail.

Result: Trading profits from foreign prop firms are **not Philippine-source** → **₱0 tax**

---

## 2. WHAT is taxed — Recharacterise Income

### Principle
Under the **CREATE Law**, **foreign-sourced dividends** received by a **domestic corporation** are **income-tax exempt** if reinvested locally within 1 year.

### Loophole B: "Dividend-Wash" via One-Person Corporation (OPC)
1. Form a **One-Person Corporation (OPC)** — you are the sole director/shareholder.
2. Have the **OPC contract with prop firms** — they pay the company, not you.
3. **Reinvest profits locally within 1 year** to qualify for the exemption (e.g., equipment, working capital).
4. Later, **declare dividends to yourself** — taxed at **10% final withholding only**.

**Effective Tax Rate**: 10% on distributions, 0% on retained earnings.

Why it works: The CREATE Law collapses the corporate tax layer via exemption, and you only pay the final 10% tax on actual cashouts.

---

## 3. HOW it's taxed — Choose the Best Tax Regime

### Principle
Philippine law allows certain taxpayers to opt into special regimes with **lower or zero tax rates** if they meet requirements.

| Regime                        | Basis                         | Eligibility Cap         | Tax Rate                     |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| 8% Flat on Gross Receipts    | TRAIN Law, §24(A)(2)          | Gross income ≤ ₱3M       | 8% of (Gross – ₱250,000)     |
| Barangay Micro Business (BMBE) | RA 9178                    | Assets ≤ ₱3M             | 0% for 2 years (renewable)   |
| Reduced Corporate Tax        | CREATE Law + DoF regulations  | Net income ≤ ₱5M & assets ≤ ₱100M | 20%                   |

### Loophole C: Register as a BMBE (if assets ≤ ₱3M)
- Trading counts as a **service business**.
- Once registered, income is **tax-free for 2 years** (renewable with conditions).
- Requires DTI registration, BIR certification, and LGU clearance.

### Loophole D: Elect the 8% Flat Tax (if gross ≤ ₱3M)
- Apply via **BIR Form 1901 / 1701Q** in Q1 of the year.
- Tax is 8% of (gross income – ₱250,000).
- Simpler filing, no deductions required.

---

## 5. Compliance Notes

- **Substance matters**: Keep documentation for OPC activities, reinvestments, and board resolutions.
- **Foreign income declaration**: NRC status must be documented; otherwise, you must declare and possibly claim foreign tax credits.
- **VAT and percentage tax**: Prop firm payouts are export services → typically 0% VAT, but still require filing.
- **AML checks**: Large USD flows may be flagged. Maintain broker contracts and fund flow records.

## Decision Matrix

| Situation                                | Best Strategy                      |
|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Living abroad > 183 days/year            | Non-Resident Citizen (₱0 tax)      |
| Income > ₱3M, but based in PH            | OPC + CREATE (≈10% effective tax)  |
| Assets < ₱3M                             | BMBE (0% for 2 years)              |
| Gross income ≤ ₱3M                       | 8% Flat Tax                        |
